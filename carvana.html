Carvana - E-Commerce Auto Retailer Gone Off the Cliff

Vansh Sharma October 02, 2022 · 10 min read

Carvana Co., together with its subsidiaries, operates an e-commerce platform for buying and selling used cars in the United States. The company's platform allows customers to research and identify a vehicle; inspect it usingcompany's 360-degree vehicle imaging technology; obtain financing and warranty coverage; purchase the vehicle; and schedule delivery or pick-up from their desktop or mobile devices. Carvana Co. was founded in 2012 and is headquartered in Tempe, Arizona.

The business combines a comprehensive online sales experience with a vertically integrated supply chain that allows them to sell high-quality vehicles to the customers transparently and efficiently at a low price. Using the website, customers can complete all phases of a used vehicle purchase transaction.

1) Purchase a Used Vehicle – Listed approximately 78,900 total units on the website, where customers can select and purchase a vehicle, including arranging financing and signing contracts, directly from their desktop or mobile device. Selling used vehicles to retail customers is the primary driver of Carvana.

2) Finance a Purchase – Customers can pay for their Carvana vehicle using cash, financing from other parties such as banks or credit unions, or financing with us using our proprietary loan origination platform. Customers who choose to apply for our in-house financing fill out a short prequalification form, select from a range of financing terms they provide and, if approved, apply the financing to their purchase in our online checkout process.

3) Protect the Purchase – Customers have the option to protect their vehicle with a VSC as part of the online checkout process. VSCs provide customers with insurance against certain mechanical repairs after the expiration of their vehicle’s original manufacturer warranty. Carvana earn a fee for selling VSCs on behalf of DriveTime, who is the obligor under these VSCs.

4) Sell a Used Vehicle – Allow customers to trade-in a vehicle and apply the tradein value to their purchase, or to sell us a vehicle independent of a purchase. Using our digital appraisal tool, customers can receive a firm offer for their vehicle nearly instantaneously from our site simply by answering a few questions about the vehicle condition and features.

Potential in Used Cars Industry: The used car market has been gaining steam since 2017 when the volume of used light vehicles sold hiked by just under 11 percent year-over-year to 42.7 million sales. By 2021, around 43.1 million used light vehicles were sold in the U.S, representing just under 74 percent of the total light vehicle sales in the country. While sales increased, the average used vehicle selling price also inflated (up 21 percent year-over-year in 2021) with vans, sport-utility vehicles, and crossover utility vehicles recording the steepest price increase. The semiconductor shortage, forcing halts in new vehicle production, is partly responsible for this boom in the used car market. Most used vehicle retail inventory in June 2022 was priced above 35,000 U.S. dollars, making the used car market increasingly inaccessible to consumers.



Pricing and Market Share: An Average Selling Price for a Used Vehicle is $25.1k (Increase by 8.5% from previous year). For Wholesale Vehicles, an average selling price is $12.4k (Increase by 7.5% from previous year). Carvana has 10.28% Market Share in of the total $138.1B Used Car Industry in 2021. I believe that they can pull off 25% Market Share in the next 10 years. Carvana sold 117,564 used vehicles and 55,299 wholesale vehicles from Mar 31, 2022 to Jun 30, 2022. The increase in revenue was primarily driven by the acquisition of ADESA, resulting in 111,883 wholesale marketplace units sold and $108 million in wholesale revenue.

Key Risks and Concerns (Seasonality): Used vehicle sales generally exhibit seasonality with sales peaking late in the first calendar quarter and diminishing through the rest of the year, with the lowest relative level of vehicle sales expected to occur in the fourth calendar quarter. Due to Carvana's rapid growth, our overall sales patterns to date have not reflected the general seasonality of the used vehicle industry, but Carvana expect this to change once the business and markets mature. Used vehicle prices also exhibit seasonality, with used vehicles depreciating at a faster rate in the last two quarters of each year and a slower rate in the first two quarters of each year, all other factors being equal.

Government Regulations (Environment): Used Vehicles usually are less environment friendly and, therefore, there are more restrictions by the US government related to environment regarding material storage, air quality, operation of gasoline storage tanks, gasoline dispensing equipment, oil tanks, and paint booths among other things.

Competitive Advantages: Although, Carvana has mentioned competitive advantages like "Purpose-Built Vertically Integrated Ecommerce Platform", "Differentiated Shopping Experience", "Proprietary Financing Technology", "Efficient Logistics Network", "Scaled Used Vehicle Infrastructure" and "Powerful Network Effects". But, I don't think any of them are strong and sustainable competitive advantages for Carvana. They need something else to establish themselves differently from other.

Growth Strategies (Mobile Sales Platform): Carvana is investing in a mobile platform to enhance customers’ ability to search for, research, finance, sell, and purchase vehicles entirely on mobile devices, including smartphones and tablets. According to J.D. Power 2017 New Autoshopper Study, 56% of auto research occurred on a mobile device. According to Statista, goods and services purchased via mobile devices by U.S. consumers totaled $338 billion in 2020, which accounted for 31% of total e-commerce. Growth in mobile-only sales depends on our ability to deliver an innovative, appealing mobile experience, as well as customers’ tastes for buying exclusively on mobile devices.

Revenues to Profits: Cost of Sales are associated with acquiring the vehicle and preparing it for sale (87% of the revenues). As selling used cars is not going to be a high margin business, Carvana needs to find a way to make more sales, while at the same time, keep the cost of acquiring vehicles to a minimum. I expect them to make 9.78% Operating Margins (the 90th percentile of industry). Based on the debt they have on their balance sheet, 4-5% Operating Margins are not going to pull out Carvana from Bankruptcy. It's wishful thinking on my part, to give Carvana 9.78%.



Competition: The U.S. used car marketplace is highly fragmented. There are approximately 43,000 used car dealerships in the United States according to Borrell Associates' 2017 Outlook, including approximately 27,000 independent dealerships. The largest dealer brand commands approximately 2.2% of the U.S. market, according to Automotive News. The leading U.S. automotive retailing and services company is AutoNation with revenues of $8.64B. Best-selling used car model from MY2016 to MY2020 is Ford F-150.

Valuation Summary

Based on the key pointers and fundamentals, here is my summary of my valuation of Carvana.

Discounted Cash Flow Analysis: My base case analysis, with a Discount Rate of 29.61% based on WACC based on CAPM (Capital Asset Pricing Model) and a Terminal FCF Growth Rate of 2%, produced an implied share price of $21. With a probability of bankruptcy of 88.14%, the adjusted fair value per share is $2.49.

Equity as an Option Analysis: My DCF Calculation is based on taking the Market Value of Debt rather than Book Value of Debt. Carvana has no value when the book value of debt is taken into consideration. In that case, the only viable way to value Carvana is to value it’s equity as an option via Black Scholes Option Pricing Model. With a probability of bankruptcy of 25.58%, the adjusted fair value per share is $10.02.

Current Share Price: At $20.30, Carvana appears substantially overvalued relative to the implied intrinsic value from my discounted cash flow (DCF) analysis and Equity as an Option Analysis. I believe a share price closer to $2.49 (in case of DCF) and $10.02 (in case of Equity as an option) would be more in-line with my valuation methodologies.

Other Methodologies: I do not view Precedent Transactions as meaningful due to the overly broad selection criteria as well as the lack of truly comparables deals; the public markets have also changed substantially in the past year, and the M&A transactions, many of which are from 3-5 years ago, do not reflect current market conditions.

Target Price: As a result, I selected $14.45 as my target price for my sell recommendation. Noting the fact that, the target price is based on 12 months.

DCF Valuation

Market Share: Carvana has 10.28% market share of its gross industry and I project them to take 25% in the next years. Although the Used Car Market is highly fragmented and it’s difficult to pull off 25%, but based on the fact that their overall focus is on revenues with their growth strategies, I give them 25%. Overall, I projected their Sales to grow at 13.67% CAGR for the next 10 years.

Operating Margin: Carvana has -2.30% Operating Margins as of June 2022. I project them to improve it to 9.78% (90th percentile of the industry) or 7.51%, taken into account the Equity Options and Restricted Stocks value in the future (which are treated as Operating Expenses).

Reinvestment: Rather than creating CapEx and Working Capital Schedules, I projected the total reinvestment based on the revenues Carvana will be able to pull off. Carvana makes $2.03 for every $1 of Invested Capital in 2021. However, the 50th percentile of gross industry makes $1.58 for every $1 of Invested Capital in 2021.

ROIC: I expect Carvana to improve their ROIC from -4.66% to 12.04% in the next 10 years, given that if they are able to make it to the 10th year.

Risk Free Rate: The Risk Free Rate is the 10 Year US Government Bond Yield.

Carvana’s ERP: As Carvana’s revenues and operations are purely based in the US, I took the US Equity Risk Premium (October 2022) of 5.30%.

Beta: Although Regression Beta (5 Years, Monthly) is 2.69, but I used the bottom up approach to find out the beta of Carvana, given that Carvana has substantial amount of debt on its balance sheet.







With these assumptions and the Free Cash Flow projections above, I came up with a fair value of $2.49 per share. Carvana's Stock was trading at $20.30 per share (October 02, 2022).

Spotify - Unleash the Beat

Vansh Sharma March 03, 2023 · 10 min read

Spotify is a digital music streaming service that allows users to access a vast collection of music and podcasts from around the world. The company operates on a freemium model, which means that users can use the service for free with some limitations or pay a monthly subscription fee for additional features and benefits. Spotify generates revenue through three primary sources:

A. Premium subscriptions: Users can pay a monthly fee to access premium features such as ad-free listening, offline playback, and higher audio quality.

B. Advertising: Spotify offers a free version of its service that is supported by ads. Advertisers can target users based on their location, demographics, and listening habits.

C. Partnerships and licensing: Spotify has partnerships with various companies, including Uber, Hulu, and Samsung, which provide additional revenue streams. Additionally, the company pays licensing fees to music labels and publishers for the right to stream their content.

User acquisition: Spotify uses a variety of strategies to acquire new users, including word-of-mouth referrals, social media marketing, and partnerships with artists and influencers.

Content curation: Spotify invests heavily in its recommendation algorithms to provide users with personalized playlists and recommendations based on their listening habits.

Cost management: To ensure profitability, Spotify focuses on cost management by optimizing its operations, negotiating favorable licensing deals with music labels, and minimizing marketing expenses.



Spotify's business model is focused on offering a high-quality music streaming service to users while generating revenue through subscription fees, advertising, and partnerships. The company's success is largely dependent on its ability to acquire new users, retain existing ones, and provide a compelling user experience.

Company’s Strengths: (Large user base) Spotify has a significant user base with over 365 million active monthly users as of September 2021, which provides a strong foundation for its business model. (Strong brand reputation) Spotify is a well-known and respected brand in the music industry, with a reputation for providing a high-quality music streaming service. (Personalized music recommendations) Spotify's recommendation algorithms are highly advanced, allowing it to provide personalized playlists and music recommendations for each user. (Diverse content) Spotify offers a wide range of content, including music, podcasts, and audiobooks, which appeals to a broad audience.

Company’s Weaknesses: (Dependence on music labels) Spotify is heavily dependent on music labels for content, which can lead to disputes over licensing fees and other issues. (Limited geographic reach) While Spotify is available in many countries, it is not available in all regions, limiting its potential user base. (Reliance on advertising revenue) Spotify's advertising revenue is subject to fluctuations due to changes in the advertising market, which could impact its profitability. (High royalty costs) Spotify pays a significant amount in royalties to music labels and publishers, which can impact its profitability.

Potential Opportunities: (Expansion into new markets) Spotify can continue to expand into new markets to increase its user base and revenue streams. (Development of original content) Spotify can create its own original content, such as exclusive podcasts and music, to differentiate itself from competitors. (Collaboration with artists) Spotify can collaborate with artists to create exclusive content, such as live performances or behind-the-scenes interviews, to engage users. (Partnerships with other companies) Spotify can form partnerships with other companies to provide additional benefits to its users and increase revenue streams.

Threats that company might face: (Competition) Spotify faces intense competition from other music streaming services such as Apple Music, Amazon Music, and YouTube Music, which could impact its market share. (Changing consumer preferences) Consumer preferences can change quickly, and Spotify must adapt to these changes to remain relevant. (Economic conditions) Economic downturns or recessions could impact Spotify's advertising revenue and subscriber growth. (Legal and regulatory issues) Spotify is subject to regulations related to music licensing, data privacy, and copyright laws, which could impact its operations and profitability.



Competitors: (Apple Music) A music streaming service offered by Apple, which offers a vast library of songs, curated playlists, and personalized recommendations. (Amazon Music) A music streaming service offered by Amazon, which offers a library of over 70 million songs, curated playlists, and personalized recommendations. (Tidal) A music streaming service owned by Jay-Z, which offers high-quality audio and exclusive content from artists. (Deezer) A music streaming service which offers a library of over 73 million songs, as well as curated playlists and personalized recommendations. (Pandora) A music streaming service which offers personalized radio stations based on your favorite artists and genres. (YouTube Music) A music streaming service offered by Google, which offers a vast library of songs, as well as music videos, live performances, and personalized playlists. (SoundCloud) A music streaming service which focuses on emerging artists and allows users to upload and share their own music. (iHeartRadio) A music streaming service which offers live radio stations, as well as curated playlists and personalized recommendations.

Corporate Governance: The CEO (Daniel Ek) of Spotify is also the Chairman of Board of Directors (which is not good in itself). The chairman of the board has the power to discipline the CEO, but in Spotify's case, that cannot happen as it's the same person!

Valuation Summary

Based on the key pointers and fundamentals, here is my summary of my valuation of Spotify.

Discounted Cash Flow Analysis: My base case analysis, with a Discount Rate of 10.80% based on Cost of Equity based on CAPM (Capital Asset Pricing Model) and a Terminal FCF Growth Rate of 2%, produced an implied share price of €107.06.

Equity Options and RSUs: Spotify issues about €464.6 million worth of equity options each financial year which I treated as operating expense in forecasted period for next 10 years.

Current Share Price: At €107.06, Spotify appears fair to the implied intrinsic value from my valuation methodologies.

Other Methodologies: I do not view Precedent Transactions as meaningful due to the overly broad selection criteria as well as the lack of truly comparables deals; the public markets have also changed substantially in the past year, and the M&A transactions, many of which are from 3-5 years ago, do not reflect current market conditions.

Target Price: As a result, I selected €119.10 as my target price for my hold recommendation. Noting the fact that, the target price is based on 12 months.

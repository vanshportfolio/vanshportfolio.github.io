Airbnb - Revolutionizing the way we “Vacation”

Vansh Sharma Febuary 10, 2023 · 10 min read

Airbnb is a platform that allows people to rent out their homes or other properties to travelers. The company was founded in 2008 and has since become one of the largest players in the travel industry. Airbnb operates on a business model where individuals list their properties on the platform, and travelers can search and book these properties for short-term stays. The company earns revenue by charging a service fee to both the property owners and the travelers for each booking made through the platform. This fee is typically around 3% for the property owner and between 6% and 15% for the traveler, depending on the booking price.

In addition to its core home-sharing business, Airbnb has expanded into other areas such as experiences (offering unique activities hosted by local guides) and hotel booking. The company has also made efforts to improve the quality and consistency of its listings through initiatives such as Airbnb Plus and Superhost status. Overall, Airbnb has disrupted the traditional hotel industry by offering a unique and often more personal alternative for travelers. The business continues to grow and expand globally, making it a significant player in the travel and tourism industry.

Airbnb acts as a broker and receives a commission from both the guests and hosts in return for its services. The basic business model of Airbnb involves three main parties.

Hosts: Individuals or property owners who list their properties on Airbnb for short-term rental.

Guests: People who are looking for accommodation for their travels and find a suitable property on Airbnb.

Commission: The platform that connects hosts and guests, provides the booking and payment infrastructure, and takes a percentage of the booking value as a commission.

In this way, Airbnb earns its revenue by charging a service fee from both the hosts and guests for every booking made through its platform.

Vacation Rentals: The global vacation rental market size was valued at USD 74.64 billion in 2021 and is expected to expand at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 5.3% from 2022 to 2030. Rising expenditure on travel, vacations, and accommodation among the millennials generation is driving the market. According to Copyrise, 200,000 million global tourists are millennials and they spend around USD 180 billion on travel every year. As the economy continues to grow, more people are looking for ways to save money on their vacations. One option is to visit vacation rental properties.



In recent years, this industry has seen incredible growth, as more and more people are looking to stay in accommodations that are both convenient and affordable. There are several reasons why the vacation rental market is growing so rapidly. First of all, there is a growing demand for unique vacation destinations that are not available domestically. This is especially true for those who want to experience the local culture while they're on vacation. Additionally, there is a growing trend of people wanting to stay closer to major metropolitan areas, rather than traveling out of town to find a vacation rental property.

Company’s Strengths: (Large and growing user base) - Airbnb has a large number of users, both hosts and guests, and a strong brand recognition globally. (Unique offering) - Airbnb offers unique and locally-situated accommodation options, which sets it apart from traditional hotels and resorts. (Strong technology platform) - Airbnb has a robust technology platform that supports easy property search, booking, and payment processes for guests and efficient management of listings for hosts.

Company’s Weaknesses: (Regulatory challenges) - Airbnb faces regulatory challenges in several cities and countries, including zoning laws, taxes, and insurance requirements, which could limit its growth potential. (Quality control issues) - Airbnb has faced quality control issues, including incidents of fraud, property damage, and safety concerns, which could harm its reputation. (Dependence on property owners) - Airbnb's business model is dependent on property owners who list their properties on its platform, and it may face challenges in retaining them and attracting new ones.

Potential Opportunities: (Expansion into new markets) - Airbnb has the opportunity to expand into new markets and diversify its offerings, such as experiences, transport, and other travel services. (Partnerships and acquisitions) - Airbnb can partner with other companies and acquire startups to enhance its offerings and strengthen its market position. (Increased demand for alternative accommodations) - With the growing popularity of alternative travel options, Airbnb has the opportunity to benefit from increased demand for short-term rental properties.

Threats that company might face: (Competition from established players) - Airbnb faces intense competition from established players in the travel and hospitality industry, including hotels, online travel agencies, and home-sharing platforms. (Economic downturns) - Airbnb's business is vulnerable to economic downturns, as reduced consumer spending on travel and tourism could lead to decreased demand for its services. (Legal and regulatory risks) - Airbnb faces legal and regulatory risks, such as lawsuits, fines, and increased regulations, which could increase its operating costs and harm its reputation.

Competitors: (Booking.com) - A popular online travel booking platform that offers a wide range of accommodations, including hotels, vacation rentals, and apartments. (Expedia Group) - A global online travel company that operates several travel booking websites, including Hotels.com, Vrbo, and Travelocity. (TripAdvisor) - An online travel site that provides reviews and comparisons of hotels, flights, and rental properties. (HomeAway) - A vacation rental platform that offers a wide selection of properties, including apartments, cabins, and entire homes. (Agoda) - An online travel booking platform that specializes in providing travelers with a wide selection of accommodations in Asia and around the world.



(VacationRentals.com) - A vacation rental platform that allows travelers to search for and book properties for short-term stays. (FlipKey) - A vacation rental platform that offers a wide selection of properties, including apartments, condos, and entire homes. (Wimdu) - A short-term rental platform that offers a wide selection of properties, including apartments, rooms, and entire homes, in cities around the world. However, it's worth noting that Airbnb also faces competition from traditional hotel chains and local rental properties that offer short-term stays.

Valuation Summary

Based on the key pointers and fundamentals, here is my summary of my valuation of Airbnb.

Discounted Cash Flow Analysis: My base case analysis, with a Discount Rate of 11.05% based on Cost of Equity based on CAPM (Capital Asset Pricing Model) and a Terminal FCF Growth Rate of 2%, produced an implied share price of $43.36.

Equity Options: Airbnb issues about $2 billion worth of equity options each financial year which I treated as operating expense in forecasted period for next 10 years.

Normalized Operating Profit: Operating Profit has been normalized by using the last 4 years of income statement data and median operating profit margins.

Current Share Price: At $114.94, Airbnb Inc. appears significantly overvalued to the implied intrinsic value from my valuation methodologies.

Other Methodologies: I do not view Precedent Transactions as meaningful due to the overly broad selection criteria as well as the lack of truly comparables deals; the public markets have also changed substantially in the past year, and the M&A transactions, many of which are from 3-5 years ago, do not reflect current market conditions.

Target Price: As a result, I selected $48.15 as my target price for my sell recommendation. Noting the fact that, the target price is based on 12 months.

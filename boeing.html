Boeing - Can they weather the storm?

Vansh Sharma April 24, 2023 · 10 min read

Boeing is a multinational corporation that designs, manufactures, and sells airplanes, rockets, satellites, and other aerospace and defense-related products and services.

Product Development: Boeing invests heavily in research and development to design new products and improve existing ones. This involves creating new technologies and innovative products that meet the changing needs of its customers.

Manufacturing: Boeing operates a global network of manufacturing facilities that produce commercial and military aircraft, as well as other aerospace products. It uses advanced manufacturing technologies to increase efficiency and reduce costs.

Sales and Marketing: Boeing employs a range of sales and marketing strategies to promote its products and services to potential customers. This includes developing relationships with airlines, governments, and other organizations, as well as participating in trade shows and other industry events.

After-Sales Support: Boeing provides comprehensive after-sales support to its customers, including maintenance, repair, and overhaul services. It also offers training programs to help customers operate and maintain their Boeing products.

Supply Chain Management: Boeing manages a complex global supply chain, working with suppliers and partners to ensure the timely delivery of high-quality components and materials.

Financial Management: Boeing manages its finances carefully, balancing its investments in research and development with the need to generate profits for its shareholders. It also manages risks associated with the aerospace and defense industry, such as fluctuations in fuel prices and changes in government regulations.



Company’s Strengths: Strong brand recognition and reputation in the aerospace industry. Wide range of products and services, including commercial and military aircraft, satellites, and defense-related products. Strong research and development capabilities, leading to innovative new products and technologies. Large global footprint with manufacturing facilities and operations in many countries. Strong relationships with major customers, including airlines and governments.

Company’s Weaknesses: Dependence on the commercial airline industry, which can be subject to economic downturns and changes in consumer behavior. High level of competition in the aerospace industry, particularly from Airbus. High debt levels due to investments in new products and technologies. Safety concerns and issues with the 737 MAX aircraft.

Potential Opportunities: Growing demand for commercial air travel in emerging markets. Expansion into the space exploration and tourism markets. Increasing demand for unmanned aerial vehicles (drones) for both military and civilian applications. Opportunities for growth through mergers and acquisitions.

Threats that company might face: Economic downturns and changes in consumer behavior affecting the commercial airline industry. Competition from Airbus and other aerospace companies. Government regulations and tariffs affecting international trade. Safety concerns and issues with the 737 MAX aircraft.



Competitors: (Airbus) The leading competitor to Boeing in the global aviation industry. (Embraer) Brazilian aircraft manufacturer specializing in regional and business jets. (COMAC) China's state-owned commercial aircraft manufacturer, aiming to challenge Boeing and Airbus. (Bombardier) Canadian company producing a range of commercial and business aircraft. (Sukhoi Aviation Corporation) Russian manufacturer of military and civilian aircraft, including the Superjet 100 passenger plane. (Mitsubishi Aircraft Corporation) Japanese manufacturer of regional jets such as the Mitsubishi SpaceJet. (Textron Aviation) American company producing business jets and turboprop aircraft. (General Electric) A key supplier of aircraft engines to many commercial airlines, including those operating Boeing planes.

Corporate Governance: The CEO (David L. Calhoun) of Boeing is also the Member of Board of Directors.

Valuation Summary

Based on the key pointers and fundamentals, here is my summary of my valuation of Boeing.

Discounted Cash Flow Analysis: My base case analysis, with a Discount Rate of 9.62% based on Cost of Equity based on CAPM (Capital Asset Pricing Model) and a Terminal FCF Growth Rate of 1%, produced fair value of $13.15.

Equity Options: Boeing issues about $182 million worth of equity options each financial year which I treated as operating expense in forecasted period for next 10 years.

Current Share Price: At $205.77, Boeing appears highly overvalued to the implied intrinsic value from my valuation methodologies.

Other Methodologies: As per Precedent Transactions as Enterprise Value to Sales, the fair value range comes from $111 and $184.

Target Price: As a result, I selected $14.34 as my target price for my sell recommendation. Noting the fact that, the target price is based on 12 months.
